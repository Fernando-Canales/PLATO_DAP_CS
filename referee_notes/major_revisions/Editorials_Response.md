### First editorial
>	The paper should refer to “asteroseismology” rather than “seismic techniques”.

### Second editorial
> 	In the opening paragraph of the introduction, the paper states that almost 5000 exoplanets have been discovered. This is an underestimate; depending on which catalogue you check, the number is either nearly 6000 or nearly 7500. Given the differences between the criteria that different catalogues used for including an exoplanet, and rate at which the number of known exoplanets is increasing, it is important to state when and where your numbers are from. Please check your number, state your source, and give the date that you accessed it. 

### Third editorial
>	In the introduction, on page 1, right-hand column, the paper states that “PLATO will increase the detection of Earth-like planets”. This statement is ambiguous. What is the intended meaning here. Is that a) PLATO will increase the number of such planets detected, b) that the detection rate will be increased, or c) that the ability to detect such planets will be improved? Please clarify the wording here.

### Fourth editorial
>	 In the introduction, on page 2, the paper refers to “transit surveys” and “space missions”. I would point out that Kepler and TESS are transit surveys, they just happen to be in space. Reference to “ground-based transit surveys” and “space-based transit surveys” would be better.

### Fifth editorial
> 	In the introduction, page 2, left-hand column, the paper defined validation as making a transit signal meet one or more criteria that indicate likely planetary statement. This sounds as though the transit signal is being manipulated to make it meet the criteria, when really the transit signal is being assessed against those criteria. This statement should be reworded to make the meaning clearer.

### Sixth editorial
> 	In the introduction, page 2, left-hand column, the paper notes that the size of the P5 sample will be ~122500 stars. Is this for one long pointing or two long pointings?

### Seventh editorial
>	 In section 2.1, Marchiori et al. (2019) is used as the reference for PLATO’s quarterly rotation. Would one of the Rauer et al. papers not be a more suitable reference here?

### Eighth editorial
> 	In section 2.4, on page 4 (left-hand column), the paper states that transit depth is the fraction of the star surface that is covered during the transit. This is incorrect, but I think is just imprecise wording. Transit depth is the fraction of the area of the stellar disc that is covered, not the fraction of the star’s surface area. 

### Ninenth editorial
> 	In section 4, on page 6, left-hand column, the paper states that centroid measurements are weighted by the aperture. This statement should be clarified; weighted how, and why? 

### Tenth editorial
> 	A citation should be added for the microscanning process referenced at the end of section 5.3

### Eleventh editorial
> 	I found sections 6.2.1, 6.2.2, and 6.2.3 somewhat confusing in terms of which contaminants were considered in each case. Figure 1 could be used to give some examples here.

### Twelfth editorial
> 	The percentages given for the number of P5 targets with certain numbers of contaminants are not completely consistent between the abstract (22% of stars have 2+ false positives) and section 7 (21%). Moreover, the percentages given in section 7 only add up to 96%. Figure 3 suggests that both of these are rounding errors, but this should be checked and the abstract and section 7 made consistent


### Thirteenth editorial
> 	 Why is the efficiency for the secondary centroid shift measurement in Table 2 quoted to a different precision than the other values (which are all quoted to the same precision)? This should be consistent
